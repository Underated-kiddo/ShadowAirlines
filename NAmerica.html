Culture

North America is culturally diverse, especially in the United States and Canada, with influences from indigenous peoples, European settlers, African Americans, and waves of immigrants from around the world.,
In the United States, culture is often characterized by individualism, innovation, and a strong sense of national identity, with an emphasis on freedom and opportunity.
The country has a significant influence on global pop culture through its media, fashion, and technology. The U.S. is also known for its iconic music genres like jazz, rock, and hip-hop.
Canada, while similar, has a strong emphasis on bilingualism, with both English and French as official languages. Its multiculturalism and commitment to social welfare are key features of Canadian culture.
Both countries are known for their sports, such as American football, baseball, hockey, and basketball, and their food, which ranges from fast food to international cuisines.
