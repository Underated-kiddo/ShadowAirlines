Culture

South American culture is shaped by a mix of indigenous traditions and influences from European colonization, especially Spanish and Portuguese, along with African heritage due to the transatlantic slave trade.
Countries like Brazil, Argentina, and Peru have their own distinct cultural traits. In Brazil, for example, Carnival, samba music, and football are deeply rooted in the culture. Argentina is known for its passion for soccer and tango dancing, while Peru is renowned for its ancient Incan heritage, with the famous Machu Picchu as a symbol of its historical richness.
South American cuisine is equally diverse, with dishes like Argentine asado (barbecue), Brazilian feijoada (bean stew), and Peruvian ceviche (raw fish) taking center stage.
