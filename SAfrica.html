Culture

South African culture is deeply influenced by its history of apartheid and the multicultural society that emerged post-apartheid. The country is often referred to as the "Rainbow Nation" due to its diverse population of over 11 official languages, including Zulu, Xhosa, Afrikaans, and English.
South Africa's music, art, and dance are influenced by African, European, and Asian cultures, with styles like Kwaito and traditional drumming being popular.
South African cuisine is also diverse, with bobotie (a spiced minced meat dish), bunny chow (a curry-filled bread), and braai (barbecue) being some iconic foods.
Sport plays a major role in national identity, with rugby, soccer, and cricket being the most popular. South Africa’s wildlife and national parks, like Kruger National Park, are central to the country's tourism.
