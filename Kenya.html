Culture

Kenya's culture is a blend of traditional African values and modern influences, shaped by its rich ethnic diversity.
With more than 40 ethnic groups, the country has a vibrant mix of languages, including Swahili and English, which are both official languages.
The Kikuyu, Luo, Maasai, Luhya, and Kalenjin are some of the major ethnic groups. Traditional music, dance, and art remain an important part of Kenyan culture, with drumming and storytelling playing a key role.
The Maasai, known for their distinctive clothing and beadwork, are particularly famous. Kenyan cuisine is diverse, with dishes like nyama choma (grilled meat), ugali (maize meal), and sukuma (collard greens) being staples.
Religion is also significant, with Christianity and Islam being the dominant religions, though traditional beliefs still play a role in some communities.
