Culture

Australia’s culture is a mix of Indigenous traditions and modern Western influences, particularly British, due to its colonial history. The Indigenous Aboriginal and Torres Strait Islander peoples have a rich cultural heritage, with traditions such as dreamtime stories, ceremonies, and art.
Modern Australia is known for its multiculturalism, with waves of immigrants from Europe, Asia, and other parts of the world contributing to a diverse cultural landscape.
Australians are often known for their laid-back lifestyle and love for outdoor activities such as surfing, sports, and barbecues. Major sporting events like the Melbourne Cup and AFL are deeply embedded in the culture.
The national cuisine is influenced by British heritage but has also incorporated flavors from Asia and the Mediterranean, with popular dishes like meat pies, Vegemite, and seafood.
